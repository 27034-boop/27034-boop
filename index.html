Common AI Legal Risks

Many companies use AI before fully understanding the risks. These are some of the most common issues Law for AI helps address.

Data Privacy Problems

Entering sensitive customer, employee, or business data into AI tools may create privacy or confidentiality concerns.

Biased Outputs

AI systems can produce biased or discriminatory outcomes, especially in hiring, lending, insurance, and customer decisions.

Incorrect Information

AI tools can generate confident but inaccurate answers, creating risk if businesses rely on them without review.

IP & Ownership Issues

Questions may arise around who owns AI-generated work and whether training or output uses protected material.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need AI legal help if I only use ChatGPT or other tools?

Yes, especially if employees enter private data, customer information, contracts, financial details, or confidential business information into AI tools.

Can Law for AI help startups?

Yes. Startups can get help with product launch risk, terms of service, privacy concerns, vendor agreements, and AI compliance planning.

Can you create an internal AI policy?

Yes. Internal AI policies explain what tools employees may use, what data they may enter, and when human review is required.

Is this only for technology companies?

No. Any business using AI for marketing, sales, hiring, customer support, analytics, or operations can benefit from AI legal guidance.
